Present Indian Scenario

India loses around Rs. 24000 crores every year on account of corrosion and enormous number of infrastructures along with cost of Indian economy, which are corroding in crying need of repairing and rehabilitation. Rebuilding them we would mean it in decreased expenditure. We need to fight against corrosion using corrosion protection techniques; the latest techniques are now available in our country.

Metals are mostly in the form of their oxides, sulfates, chlorides, silicates, basic carbonates etc... Almost every metal surface comes in contact with gases or liquids and the decay of metal is either due to chemical or electrochemical reactions.

Any chemical process or electrochemical reaction, which starts decay or destruction of the metal due to surrounding environment is called corrosion. The most popular type of corrosion is rusting (Iron & steel). When metal comes in contact with atmospheric air or solid-gas halogenous system and under this condition, corrosion starts, which is termed as an Atmospheric Corrosion.

Almost all machinery and other resources are partly made of steel if not fully. In order to increase industrial advancement, it is necessary to make sure that the steel is made corrosion resistant by using the right kind of protective coating.

How Corrosion Destroys

Corrosion may begin in a small way, but gradually increases in manifold as corrosion occupy 20 to 30 times the volume occupied by the original matter. Speed of expansion takes place, cracks soon appears and this further aggravates corrosion, leads to total working capacity of structure. At this stage, nothing much can be done to prevent the laiture of the structures. You have to either rehabilitate or rebuild.

To find a permanent solution to the problem of corrosion, several methods have been tried; however none of them have been so successful in preventing corrosion in the long term basis. Some methods have even aggravated the problem to some extent, but the only solution is to apply a suitable protective coating.
